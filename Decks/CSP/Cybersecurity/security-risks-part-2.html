Activity

Your Digital Self

Every time you post, comment, sign up for something, or appear on a website, you leave behind information. That collection of information is called your digital footprint.

Think Like a Researcher

Imagine someone is researching you online. What information would they find? What could they learn by connecting that information together?

In the next few slides, you are going to actually do this search on yourself and record what you find.

Activity

Your Digital Self: Conduct Your Search

Conduct Your Search

  • Start by searching your full name on a search engine.
  • Add more details to narrow the search, like your city or school.
  • Check social media, school websites, or public directories.

Record Your Findings

  • Write down any information you find about yourself.
  • Include where you found it: the website or source.
  • If you know something is online but cannot see it right now, still record it.

Based on what you found, what bigger picture could someone piece together about you?

Activity

Your Digital Self: Evaluate Your Risk

Evaluate Your Risk

  • Which piece of information seems like the biggest threat to your privacy or security?
  • Why is it risky?

See How Easy It Is

Visit aboutmyinfo.org/identity and enter just your gender, birth date, and ZIP code. See how easily small pieces of data can uniquely identify you.

FRQ

Reflecting on Your Digital Footprint

After completing this activity, how has your understanding of your digital footprint changed? What steps, if any, would you take to better protect your personal information online?

Think about what you found in your search, what surprised you, and what felt uncomfortable to see.
