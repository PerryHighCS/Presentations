Wrap Up

Key Takeaways

Privacy Powers Innovation

Our private data drives many computing innovations in ways we actually like: more convenient, personalized, and often "free" services. We pay with our data, and that tradeoff has real value on both sides.

Unintended Consequences

Not every effect of a computing innovation is anticipated in advance. Data collected for one purpose can end up being used in ways the user never agreed to or expected.

Real Harms Are Possible

This data can be used by companies, governments, or criminals in ways that threaten privacy. The same location data that helps you navigate can be used to track your movements without your knowledge.

An Ongoing Debate

The balance between innovation, privacy, and security is continually being debated. Legal and ethical concerns arise when computing innovations harm people or play a role in social and political issues. You are part of the generation that will shape those decisions.
